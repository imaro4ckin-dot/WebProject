Experiencing the Fallas Festival in Valencia

By Rodrigo | Published: March 19, 2026 | Culture

Every March, Valencia transforms into one of the most spectacular festivals in the world. Las Fallas is a celebration of fire, art, and community. The best part? Most of it is completely free to experience.

Enormous sculptures called fallas — some towering over five stories tall, are built by neighbourhood groups over months and displayed across the city for just a few days before being dramatically burned on the final night, La Cremà. Walking between them felt like wandering through an open-air museum that was about to explode.

Our Budget Breakdown

Fallas is one of the most student-friendly festivals in Europe. Here's how we kept costs low:

  • Festival entry: Free! All the street sculptures and parades are open to everyone.
  • Food: €8.00 (Buñuelos and hot chocolate from a street stall — a Fallas tradition)
  • Transport: €1.50 (One metro ride from the city centre — we walked everywhere else)
  • Accommodation: €18.00 per person (Shared hostel dorm booked weeks in advance)

Pro Travel Tip:

Book your accommodation as early as possible — Valencia fills up fast during Fallas week. Prices double or triple if you leave it to the last minute. Aim to arrive a couple days before March 19th to catch the full atmosphere.
